# Google FDE Interview Guide

## Role Overview

**Full title:** Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE)  
**Organization:** Google Cloud (Customer Engineering)  
**Levels:** L3 (entry), L4 (mid), L5 (senior), L6 (staff), L7 (principal)

Most external hires target L4 or L5. L3 is typically for new grads or early-career engineers.

---

## What Google FDEs Do

Google FDEs are embedded technical advisors for Google Cloud's most strategic customers. They:

- Design and implement production solutions on GCP
- Own customer technical relationships end-to-end
- Translate customer business problems into GCP architectures
- Influence Google Cloud product roadmap based on customer feedback
- Build repeatable solution patterns that can be scaled across accounts

**Day-to-day:** Heavy on customer interaction (calls, onsites), deep technical work (coding, architecture), and internal enablement (writing up what works, mentoring).

---

## Interview Structure

### Screening

- Recruiter phone screen (30 min)
- Hiring manager intro (30 min)
- Possible technical phone screen (45-60 min)

### Onsite (4-5 rounds)

**Round 1: DSA**
- One or two coding problems
- 45-60 minutes
- Interviewer from engineering; they evaluate code quality and communication

**Round 2: RRK (Role Related Knowledge)**
- Technical depth on GCP and AI/ML
- Customer scenario analysis
- "How would you architect X?"
- 45-60 minutes

**Round 3: Behavioral / Leadership**
- STAR format
- Google's leadership attributes (not as formalized as Amazon's LPs)
- Focus on customer impact, cross-functional work, ambiguity

**Round 4: Hiring Manager / Culture**
- Why Google? Why FDE?
- Career goals
- Values alignment

*(Some candidates have a 5th round — additional technical or customer scenario.)*

---

## What Google Values in FDE Candidates

Based on community reports:

**Technical excellence:** The bar is real. You cannot skate through on interpersonal skills. The DSA round is hard. The RRK depth is expected to be genuine.

**GCP breadth and depth:** At minimum: Vertex AI, BigQuery, Cloud Run, Pub/Sub. Bonus: Dataflow, Bigtable, Cloud Spanner, GKE.

**Customer empathy:** Can you translate business problems to technical solutions? Can you explain technical tradeoffs to a VP of Engineering?

**Ownership:** Google rewards people who see a problem and fix it without being asked.

**Communication:** You'll be speaking to customers in your first week. Interviewers are literally evaluating whether they'd want you representing Google in that conversation.

## Preparation Priorities for Google FDE

**Weeks 1-4:** DSA — two problems per day, focused on patterns. Mock interviews 2x/week.

**Weeks 5-8:** GCP deep dive — hands-on with Vertex AI, BigQuery, Cloud Run. Build something real.

**Weeks 9-12:** RRK preparation — customer scenarios, system design, practice explaining architectures out loud.

**Throughout:** Behavioral stories — write them out, practice delivering them in 2 minutes.

## Cooling Off Period

**12 months after rejection.** Plan your timeline accordingly. This is a real rule.

If you were rejected from Google FDE: use the year. Build GCP hands-on experience, get certified, do 50+ mock interviews. Come back stronger.
