# FDE at Each Company

The FDE role varies significantly across companies. Knowing the differences helps you tailor your preparation and your answers.

---

## Google FDE

**Official title:** Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE)

**What they do:**
Google FDEs are embedded with Google Cloud's largest enterprise customers. They build production solutions — not demos — using GCP services. The work is deeply technical: custom data pipelines, Vertex AI deployments, BigQuery architectures, and complex integrations.

**Who they work with:**
Fortune 500 companies, large public sector organizations, and strategic cloud migration customers. The customer engineering team at Google is one of the largest technical field organizations in the industry.

**What makes Google FDE unique:**
- Scale: the customers are enormous
- GCP-first: everything runs on Google Cloud
- Internal mobility: Google FDE is a strong path to product, research, or internal engineering
- RRK round is distinctly Google: it tests GCP architecture, not just generic system design

**Technical stack you need to know:**
Vertex AI, BigQuery, Cloud Run, Pub/Sub, GKE, Dataflow, Cloud Storage, IAM, VPC

**Levels:** L3, L4, L5, L6, L7

## Anthropic

**Official title:** Various (Software Engineer, AI Engineer, Research Engineer, Field AI Engineer)

**What they do:**
Anthropic's customer-facing engineering roles are focused on AI deployment at scale. You're helping enterprises integrate Claude, fine-tune models, implement RAG pipelines, and build reliable AI products. The technical bar is very high — you are expected to understand the AI stack deeply, not just call APIs.

**Who they work with:**
Enterprise customers across finance, legal, healthcare, and technology. The field team is small relative to peers; each engineer owns significant customer relationships.

**What makes Anthropic unique:**
- The AI depth required is the highest of any company on this list
- Constitutional AI, RLHF, and Claude's specific capabilities matter
- The interview focuses on your genuine understanding of LLMs, not just pattern-matched answers
- Culture values honesty and intellectual humility — interviewers notice when you pretend to know things

**Technical stack you need to know:**
Claude API, prompt engineering, RAG, fine-tuning, vector databases (Pinecone, Weaviate), LangChain/LlamaIndex, Python, evaluation frameworks

**See:** [05-company-specific/anthropic.md](../05-company-specific/anthropic.md)

## Meta

**Official title:** Forward Deployed Engineer

**What they do:**
Meta FDEs work with the company's largest advertising, commerce, and enterprise partners. The technical work involves Meta's AI advertising stack, the Llama ecosystem, and increasingly the Meta AI platform.

**Who they work with:**
Major advertisers, e-commerce platforms, and enterprise integrators. The scale is immense — Meta's advertising business touches billions of users.

**What makes Meta FDE unique:**
- Advertising tech is core: you need to understand Meta's advertising infrastructure
- AI is now central: Llama, Llama.cpp, PyTorch, and Meta AI products
- Scale: Meta's systems operate at a scale few companies match
- Fast-moving: the product surface changes constantly

**Technical stack you need to know:**
PyTorch, Llama ecosystem, Meta Ads API, Graph API, React (for demos), Python

---

## Amazon

**Official title:** Technical Account Manager (TAM), AI/ML Specialist Solutions Architect, or Customer Engineer (role name varies by program)

**What they do:**
Amazon's customer-facing technical roles work with AWS's strategic accounts. The work involves cloud architecture, MLOps, SageMaker deployments, and deep customer engagement.

**What makes Amazon unique:**
- Leadership Principles are heavily weighted in interviews — know them cold
- AWS is the platform: breadth of services matters
- Customer obsession is a real cultural value, not a buzzword
- More formal escalation structures than Google or Anthropic

**Technical stack you need to know:**
AWS services broadly, SageMaker, Bedrock, Lambda, ECS/EKS, RDS, S3, IAM

---

## Microsoft

**Official title:** Customer Engineer, AI Cloud Solution Architect, or Technical Specialist

**What they do:**
Microsoft's customer-facing engineers deploy Azure services — increasingly Azure AI and Copilot. The role is often more advisory (architecture reviews, POCs) than code-first.

**What makes Microsoft unique:**
- Azure AI and Copilot ecosystem are the priority focus
- More enterprise process formality than startups
- Strong partner ecosystem: a lot of work goes through Microsoft Partners
- Less "write production code at customer site" than Google FDE

**Technical stack you need to know:**
Azure AI Studio, Azure OpenAI Service, Azure ML, Azure DevOps, Power Platform
