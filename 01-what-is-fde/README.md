This section covers everything you need to understand about the role before you walk into an interview: what FDEs actually do, how the role differs across companies, how it compares to adjacent roles, and what the career path looks like.

## What Is an FDE?

An FDE sits at the intersection of engineering and customer impact. They embed directly with customers — sometimes for weeks or months — and build solutions to the hardest problems those customers face. Not demos, not integrations. Production systems.

**At Google:** FDEs work on the largest enterprise accounts. They build custom solutions on GCP, solve complex data and AI problems, and translate Google technology into measurable business outcomes.

**At Anthropic:** The role is more about AI product deployment, fine-tuning, and ensuring enterprise customers get real value from Claude. AI Engineering depth is table stakes.

**At Meta:** FDEs work with advertising, commerce, and enterprise customers. Heavy on Llama ecosystem, PyTorch, and Meta AI platform products.

**At Amazon:** Customer engineers and technical account managers who embed deeply with strategic AWS accounts. MLOps, SageMaker, and data engineering are core.

**At Microsoft:** Customer-facing engineers who deploy Azure AI services. Copilot ecosystem knowledge matters here.

## Day in the Life of an FDE (Google, L4)

A realistic week for a Google FDE:

**Monday**
- Customer sync: align on the quarter's technical priorities
- Code review: a teammate's data pipeline changes for a retail customer
- Internal: present solution architecture to account team

**Tuesday**
- Deep work: build a Vertex AI pipeline for customer use case
- Unblock customer engineering team on BigQuery permissions issue
- Write up findings from last week's architecture review

**Wednesday**
- Customer onsite (virtual): whiteboard a new recommendation system
- Document solution pattern for internal knowledge base
- 1:1 with manager about upcoming L5 opportunities

**Thursday**
- Cross-functional: work with sales on a competitive RFP response
- Write a technical blog post for the Google Cloud blog
- Review open PRs from customer's engineering team

**Friday**
- Internal: FDE team sync — share what's working and what's not
- Study time (Google provides this): GCP certification prep
- Personal project / open source contribution

**The honest version:** you are constantly context-switching between deep technical work and customer communication. You are never purely coding. You are never purely in meetings. The people who thrive in FDE roles genuinely enjoy both.

## Career Progression

```
L3 FDE I
    → Executing on defined projects
    → Learning customer dynamics
    
L4 FDE II (most new hires with 3–6 years experience)
    → Owning technical workstreams
    → Running customer relationships
    
L5 Senior FDE
    → Defining solutions, not just executing
    → Mentoring L3/L4
    → Influencing product roadmap
    
L6 Staff FDE
    → Setting technical direction across accounts
    → Cross-functional leadership
    
L7 Principal FDE
    → Strategic; rare; industry-defining work
```
