# STAR Framework for FDE Interviews

Behavioral interviews test whether you have the experience and judgment that FDE roles require. Most engineers over-engineer the technical content and under-invest in their stories.

---

## STAR Format

**S — Situation:** Set the context. 2-3 sentences.
**T — Task:** What was your specific responsibility? What were the constraints?
**A — Action:** What did YOU do? (not the team, not we — you)
**R — Result:** Quantified outcome. What changed because of your action?

---

## The FDE-Specific Stories You Need

Prepare 3-4 stories that demonstrate each of these:

### 1. Technical Leadership Under Ambiguity

*"Tell me about a time you took ownership of a complex technical problem with no clear solution."*

FDE interviewers want to see: You can operate with ambiguity, propose a direction, and execute without being told exactly what to do.

**Structure:** "The situation was undefined. Here's how I defined it, why I chose this approach, and what happened."

### 2. Customer Impact

*"Tell me about a time you directly impacted a customer's business outcomes."*

This should be your best story. The more specific the numbers, the better.

Example shape: "I identified that the customer's [problem]. I proposed and implemented [solution]. The result was [specific metric improvement] which translated to [business value]."

### 3. Cross-Functional Collaboration

*"Tell me about a time you worked across teams to deliver something."*

FDE roles require working with sales, product, customer success, and engineering simultaneously.

### 4. Technical Communication

*"Tell me about a time you explained a complex technical concept to a non-technical audience."*

FDE is fundamentally a communication role. Show you can do this well.

### 5. Handling Failure

*"Tell me about a time things went wrong. What did you do?"*

The best answers here show: you took accountability, you learned from it, you fixed the process.

---

## Preparing Your Stories

**Write them out.** Not bullet points — full STAR narratives. Typing them forces clarity.

**Quantify everything.** "Reduced latency" is weak. "Reduced p99 latency from 2.4s to 340ms, which reduced customer-reported errors by 73%" is strong.

**Make the action specific.** "I worked with the team" is weak. "I wrote the BigQuery schema design, proposed the partitioning strategy after testing 3 alternatives, and presented the tradeoffs to the customer's data team" is strong.

**Prepare 8-10 stories total.** Different questions may need different stories. Having depth means you can choose the best fit.

---

## Questions FDE Interviewers Ask

| Question | What they're testing |
|---------|---------------------|
| Tell me about yourself | Narrative coherence, why FDE |
| Why do you want this role? | Genuine motivation vs. just wanting a job |
| Tell me about a complex technical problem you solved | Technical depth + ownership |
| Tell me about a customer you helped significantly | Customer empathy + impact |
| Tell me about a time you disagreed with a technical decision | Intellectual honesty, judgment |
| What's your biggest weakness? | Self-awareness, growth mindset |
| Tell me about a time you failed | Accountability, learning |
| Where do you see yourself in 3 years? | Ambition, FDE career fit |

---

## The "Why FDE?" Question

This is the most important non-STAR question. Interviewers want to understand:
- Do you understand what FDE actually is?
- Is this a genuine career goal or a fallback?
- Do you have the temperament for customer-facing technical work?

**Strong answer elements:**
- Specific experience that made you realize you want to be at the intersection of engineering and customer impact
- Concrete understanding of what the role involves (not just "I want to help customers")
- Evidence that you've done this kind of work before and are good at it
