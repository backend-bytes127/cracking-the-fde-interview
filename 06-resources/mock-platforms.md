# Mock Interview Platforms

The difference between knowing how to solve problems and performing in an interview is practice under real conditions. Mock interviews are not optional.

---

## Free Options

### Pramp.com

**Best for:** Volume. Peer-to-peer mock interviews matched with other engineers.

- Completely free
- Matched with another engineer — you both interview each other
- Problems are curated (not random LeetCode)
- Good feedback mechanism
- Available 24/7, usually find a match within 30 minutes

**How to use it:** Book 2 sessions per week during your prep. Treat it like the real thing. Dress appropriately, use a real coding environment, time yourself.

### interviewing.io (Free Tier)

Watch recorded interviews from engineers at FAANG companies. Invaluable for seeing how others handle problems — including how they handle getting stuck.

---

## Paid Options

### interviewing.io (Paid)

Mock interviews with engineers from Google, Amazon, Meta, Anthropic, and other top companies.

- Real interviewers who've done these interviews
- Detailed post-interview feedback
- Anonymous — your performance doesn't follow you
- Expensive ($225–$350/session)
- **Worth it:** One session with a real Google interviewer reveals more than weeks of solo practice

**Recommendation:** Book one session per month during the last 3 months of prep.

### Exponent

Strong for FDE and Product roles:
- FDE-specific interview practice
- System design focus
- GCP and cloud content

Subscription model (~$20/month). Good value if you do it consistently.

### Topmate.io (India-based, works globally)

Book 1:1 sessions with senior engineers from Google, Meta, Amazon:
- Many ex-FAANG engineers available
- Pay per session (usually much cheaper than interviewing.io)
- Great for Indian engineers targeting Google, Anthropic
- Quality varies — read reviews carefully

### Meetapro

Growing community of mock interview pairs. Affordable and improving. Good for peer practice beyond Pramp.

## The Best Free Option Nobody Uses

**Record yourself on camera.**

Set a 45-minute timer. Open a blank document or online coding environment. Pick a problem you haven't solved recently. Solve it while talking out loud — out loud, not in your head.

Watch the recording back.

You will hear exactly where you go silent. You will see exactly when you start typing before you've explained your approach. You will catch every "um" and "uh" and frozen moment.

It is uncomfortable. It works. It costs nothing.

---

## Recommended Sequence

| Timeline | Activity |
|----------|----------|
| Week 1–2 | Solo recorded practice (1–2 sessions/week). Learn what you don't know. |
| Week 3–6 | Pramp 2× per week. Get comfortable with the format. |
| Month 2–3 | Pramp 3× per week + one solo recording per week. |
| Month 3+ | One paid interviewing.io or Topmate session per month. |
| Final 2 weeks | Daily practice. Mix of solo + peer mocks. |

---

## A Note on Feedback

The most valuable feedback is specific: "You went silent for 45 seconds after reading the problem" is more useful than "you seemed nervous."

Ask your mock interviewer explicitly: "Where did I lose you?" and "What would have been a stronger answer?"

Pramp's feedback forms are structured to capture this. Use them.
